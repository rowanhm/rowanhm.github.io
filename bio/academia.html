Academia. Rowan’s research explores the relationship between linguistic diversity, cultural variation, and historical change. He currently holds a Chaire d’Excellence position at Université PSL as part of CultureLab, an initiative that aims to foster collaboration in the digital humanities between members of various grande écoles in Paris (such as École normale supérieure and École nationale des chartes). Before this, he held a fellowship at Magdalene College at the University of Cambridge, where he also completed his PhD as well as his BA and MEng. His doctoral research was funded by the Leslie Wilson Vice-Chancellor's Scholarship, with additional support in the form of a Turing Institute Enrichment Award. Before moving to Paris, he spent a few months in Tokyo as the holder of a JSPS Fellowship, and before his PhD he spent a brief period at ETH Zürich as a research assistant.
